## Business Introduction

Uber operates a high-volume ride-hailing service across major UK cities, handling thousands of ride requests monthly. In a competitive and time-sensitive mobility market, leadership requires clear visibility into demand patterns, revenue drivers, and operational bottlenecks to optimize performance.

This project provides an executive-level analysis of ride activity to understand customer usage patterns, peak demand periods, revenue distribution, and service challenges impacting conversion and customer experience.

---

## Problem Statement

Uber UK lacked a consolidated performance view to answer critical operational and strategic questions:

- When is demand and revenue highest?
- Which locations generate the most revenue?
- Which ride types drive revenue mix?
- Where are requests being lost?
- What causes cancellations?
- How effective is driver supply during peak periods?

Without these insights, operational decisions risk being reactive instead of data-driven.

## Recommendations

### Improve Matching Accuracy
Strengthen driver identity verification and dispatch reliability to reduce expectation mismatches.

### Reduce Driver-Initiated Cancellations
Introduce monitoring and incentives tied to completion rates.

### Improve Service Quality
Implement targeted training and reward high-feedback drivers.

### Align Supply to Morning Peaks
Increase driver availability during morning hours where most revenue is generated.

### Reduce Accidental Requests
Improve confirmation flow and introduce short cancellation grace periods.
